# Chapter 1 — Elements of Pitch: Summary

You have covered the entire foundation of tonal music. Here is a concise map of everything in this chapter — use it as a reference before the final assessment.

## The pitch system

- Pitch is frequency. Higher frequency = higher pitch.
- The **musical alphabet** cycles: C D E F G A B C …
- **Octave registers** are numbered from C0. C4 is middle C.
- The piano is a linear pitch map; the guitar is a two-dimensional grid.
- The **12 chromatic pitches** repeat in every octave. Sharps raise by a half step; flats lower.

## Staff notation

- Five lines and four spaces. Higher = higher pitch.
- **Treble clef** pins the second line to G4.
- Guitar music is written in treble clef sounding an octave lower (indicated by the 8 subscript).
- **Grand staff:** treble above, bass below, C4 on the ledger between them.

## Scales

| Scale | Interval pattern | Degrees altered from major |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Major | W–W–H–W–W–W–H | — |
| Natural minor | W–H–W–W–H–W–W | ♭3, ♭6, ♭7 |
| Harmonic minor | W–H–W–W–H–A2–H | ♭3, ♭6 |
| Melodic minor ↑ | W–H–W–W–W–W–H | ♭3 |
| Major pentatonic | W–W–m3–W–m3 | Remove 4 and 7 |
| Minor pentatonic | m3–W–W–m3–W | Remove 2 and 6 |

## Key signatures

- A key signature places accidentals at the start of each staff line, applying them to all notes of that name.
- **Sharps:** F C G D A E B — order of addition going around the circle.
- **Flats:** B E A D G C F — the reverse.
- **Relative keys** share a key signature (e.g. C major / a minor).
- **Parallel keys** share a tonic (e.g. C major / c minor).

## Scale degree names

| Degree | Name |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1̂ | Tonic |
| 2̂ | Supertonic |
| 3̂ | Mediant |
| 4̂ | Subdominant |
| 5̂ | Dominant |
| 6̂ | Submediant |
| 7̂ | Leading tone (half step below tonic) / Subtonic (whole step) |

## Intervals

- **Generic size** counted by letter names inclusive. 
- **Quality:** Perfect (P1, P4, P5, P8), Major/Minor (2nd, 3rd, 6th, 7th), Augmented (+1 semitone), Diminished (−1 semitone).
- **Inversion rule:** numbers add to 9; qualities flip major↔minor, perfect stays perfect.
- **Tritone** (6 semitones) = A4 or d5; maximum dissonance; inverts to itself.
- **Consonance spectrum:** Perfect consonances → imperfect consonances → mild dissonance → strong dissonance → tritone.

## Guitar fretboard shapes to remember

- **Adjacent strings = P4** (except G–B = M3).
- **Same string:** 1 fret = m2, 2 frets = M2, 3 = m3, 4 = M3, 5 = P4, 7 = P5, 12 = P8.
- **Cross-string shapes shift by 1 fret at the G–B string pair.**
- **Pentatonic major:** 5 notes, no half steps, works over any major-key chord.
- **Pentatonic minor:** foundation of blues and rock.
