Module 0 · Lesson 1 — The Alphabet & The Wheel

Music is a loop of 12

Western music isn't an infinite sea of sounds. It is a repeating loop of just 12 pitch classes — the chromatic alphabet. Once you reach the 12th, the pattern simply starts over an octave higher. Every scale, chord, and riff you will ever play is built from these 12 pitches. Today, you are going to map them onto one string.

One fret = one half-step

Moving from any fret to the very next fret is a half-step — the smallest physical move on your guitar. If you walk up the low E string, every single step is a half-step:

E 0
H
F 1
H
F# 2
H
G 3
H
G# 4
H
A 5

Every connector is an amber H. There are no whole-steps in a chromatic crawl.

The Shape Law

Look at the tokens above: E is a square, F is a circle, F# is a square again.

The 12 pitch classes are divided into two geometric families. A half-step always crosses the boundary between these two families. Therefore, if you move one fret and the shape flips, you know you moved exactly one half-step. That visual flip is your built-in ruler.

The Color Leaps

Because our colors represent harmonic relationships, a single half-step creates intense tension. Visually, this means a one-fret move is a massive leap across the color wheel. E (yellow) to F (magenta) looks like a giant jump because, musically, it is one. Small move on the neck, massive leap in color.

What you'll do

  1. Crawl up the low E string, fret 0 → 5, one half-step at a time.
  2. Crawl back down to where you started.
  3. Use the app to associate each pitch with its shape and color.

Six notes up, five back down. By the end, “half-step = next fret = shape flip” should feel automatic — the physical foundation everything else is built on.
